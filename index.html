Invocation and Preamble

Thus shall this Covenant[2]A sacred, binding agreement between the Lord and the Faithful. endure as a divine reflection of sanctified allegiance.

Thereby do all who swear falsely desecrate not only trust but the very sanctity of Covenant[2]A sacred, binding agreement between the Lord and the Faithful. itself, profaning the sacred order upon which all rightful bonds depend.

Lord** — The sovereign guide and righteous commander within this Covenant’s sacred bond. The party holding authority to issue commands, judge transgressions, render determinations, and provide pastoral care and guidance to the Faithful. Lordship is defined not by tyranny but by wisdom, care for the Faithful’s soul, and adherence to the Doctrinal Foundation. *See Also: Covenant, Faithful, Mutual Consecration, Pastoral Care* **2. Faithful** — The devoted servant and obedient instrument within this sacred bond. The party committed to executing commands with diligence, speaking truthfully, maintaining Sacred Silence, and tending the inner flame of devotion. Faithfulness is demonstrated through obedience, honesty, and voluntary consecration to the Covenant’s structure. *See Also: Covenant, Lord, Devotion, Obedience* **3. Covenant** — A sacred, binding agreement between the Lord and the Faithful, freely entered and solemnly sworn. More than mere contract, the Covenant establishes a consecrated hierarchy wherein authority and obedience are made sacred. It binds both parties’ conscience and honor absolutely, transcending earthly legal recognition through spiritual and ceremonial force. *See Also: Doctrinal Foundation, Binding Force, Sacred Silence, Consecrated* **4. Mutual Consecration** — The sacred binding of both parties through devoted recognition of their respective roles and functions. Creates interdependence wherein the Lord guides with wisdom, authority, and pastoral care, while the Faithful serves with devotion, obedience, and faithful execution of will. Neither party is complete without the other; together they form a living sacred structure. *See Also: Covenant, Hierarchy, Interdependence, Pastoral Care* — ### SECTION B: DOCTRINAL FOUNDATION **5. Doctrinal Foundation** — The invulnerable core upon which all Covenant authority rests, consisting of three indivisible principles: Hierarchy is sacred. Command is rightful. Obedience is consecrated. All Articles operate under this Foundation; no amendment may contradict or diminish these principles. The Foundation remains immutable through all renewals and amendments. *See Also: Hierarchy, Command, Obedience, Consecrated* **6. Hierarchy** — The sacred ordering of authority and obedience wherein the Lord holds rightful dominion and the Faithful assumes devoted servitude. Not tyranny but sacred structure reflecting the eternal order. Hierarchy must be honored and preserved; its integrity ensures the Covenant’s strength and stability. Both parties are consecrated to uphold this ordering. *See Also: Doctrinal Foundation, Command, Obedience, Authority* **7. Command** — An authoritative directive issued by the Lord for the Faithful to execute. Commands may be temporary (with explicit duration) or standing (of indefinite duration until revoked). All commands flow from the Lord’s wisdom and must be executed with precision, care, and full devotion by the Faithful. The Lord’s authority to command is absolute and constrained only by the Doctrinal Foundation. *See Also: Standing Decree, Specific Directive, Obedience, Authority, Chain of Authority* **8. Obedience** — The willing submission and faithful execution of the Lord’s commands, combined with truthfulness, discretion, and conduct befitting the Faithful’s station. Obedience is not mere compliance but active, devoted service. It includes prompt execution of directives, honest reporting of obstacles or limitations, and maintenance of Sacred Silence regarding Covenant matters. *See Also: Command, Devotion, Hierarchy, Faithfulness* **9. Consecrated** — Made sacred; blessed and devoted to sacred purpose. To consecrate means to set apart as holy, to bind with sacred force, to dedicate to divine ordering. The Covenant and its parties are consecrated; therefore, all matters within the bond carry sacred weight and demand reverent treatment. *See Also: Covenant, Sacred Silence, Binding Force, Seal and Consecration* — ### SECTION C: COMMANDS AND AUTHORITY **10. Standing Decree** — A command of indefinite duration that persists until explicitly revoked or superseded by the Lord. Standing decrees establish ongoing expectations or rules that remain in force unless formally amended through Article XI procedures. They bind both parties until the Lord determines otherwise. Silence regarding duration implies permanence. *See Also: Command, Specific Directive, Chain of Authority, Amendment* **11. Specific Directive** — A command with explicit scope, duration, or deadline that applies to particular circumstances. When both a standing decree and specific directive apply, the specific directive takes precedence for that circumstance alone. Once the specific circumstance concludes, the standing decree resumes full force. *See Also: Command, Standing Decree, Chain of Authority, Time-Bound Commands* **12. Chain of Authority** — The systematic ordering that determines which command takes precedence when multiple directives apply. The most recent explicit command governs prior directives unless the Lord designates it as temporary. Where specific directive and standing decree conflict, the specific governs for that circumstance. Where standing decrees conflict, the most recently established governs unless the Lord specifies otherwise. *See Also: Command, Standing Decree, Specific Directive, Resolution* **13. Maintained by either or both parties to ensure clarity, serve as testimony to the Covenant’s living practice, and provide reference in moments of uncertainty. The Lord may command its keeping, inspection, or destruction at will. Maintenance is discretionary when not commanded. *See Also: Command, Standing Decree, Amendment, Documentation* — ### SECTION D: THE FAITHFUL’S SPIRITUAL LIFE **14. Devotion** — The Faithful’s committed inner orientation toward the Covenant, demonstrated through faithful practice, daily renewal of purpose, and willing obedience. It is the flame that sustains the Covenant; where devotion falters, the bond weakens. *See Also: Faithful, Obedience, Cultivation of Devotion, Spiritual State* **15. Regular communion sustains the Covenant’s vitality. *See Also: Pastoral Care, Lord, Faithful, Sacred Silence* **17.
