Article ‚Ö©: Of Transgression, Correction, and Reconciliation

Article X establishes the framework for addressing violations: classification of transgressions (Error, Breach, Profanation), enforcement principles aligning with the Foundation, and reconciliation pathways. It distinguishes ceremonial boundaries (Article VI) from ethical boundaries (binding and enforceable), and frames correction as restoration of consecrated hierarchy.

The three-tiered classification system provides proportional responses: Error (sincere mistakes) receives verbal instruction; Breach (knowing violations) receives Lord-determined correction; Profanation (rejection of hierarchy/Covenant structure) requires complete withdrawal and Formal Restoration Ceremony. Forgiveness is proportional to contrition, and correction strengthens rather than breaks the bond.
