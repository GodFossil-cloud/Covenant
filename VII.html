Of Interpretation and Counsel

§.Ⅰ Resolution shall be sought with justice and proper remembrance of station. Counsel rises upward from Faithful[1]The devoted servant and obedient instrument within this sacred bond. to Lord; it flows not laterally nor descends unbidden.
§.Ⅱ Where perception falters or judgment grows uncertain, recourse may be had to this Covenant[2]A sacred, binding agreement between the Lord and the Faithful.'s text, contemplation, sacred texts consonant with the Foundation[7]The three indivisible principles of the Covenant., or appointed witness if both parties consent.
§.Ⅲ Principles of Interpretation
Ⓐ The Lord's intent guides understanding.
Ⓑ Where ambiguity yields multiple interpretations of equitable merit, let interpretation favor that which most harmoniously fulfills the intent of the Lord.
Ⓒ Literal reading serves as baseline, but context and purpose prevail where ambiguity exists.
Ⓓ All interpretations must align with the Doctrinal Foundation[7]The three indivisible principles of the Covenant.'s principles.
Ⓔ Prior sanctioned interpretations guide present questions.
Ⓕ Where conflicts arise, the most recent explicit statement takes precedence.
Ⓖ Good faith effort shall weigh in mitigation of corrective judgment for misunderstandings.
§.Ⅳ Disputes of Fact
Where disagreement exists regarding whether a command[4]An authoritative directive given by the Lord. was issued, completed, or what was specifically commanded, resolution proceeds with mutual recitation of facts and consultation of any recorded commands[4]An authoritative directive given by the Lord..
