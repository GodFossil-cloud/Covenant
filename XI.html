Of Amendments and Renewal

§.Ⅰ Petition and Ratification
Either party may petition for revision, clarification, or repeal. All ratification rests with the Lord's authority. Upon ratification, both parties shall acknowledge acceptance in writing.
§.Ⅱ The Covenant[2]A sacred, binding agreement between the Lord and the Faithful. shall be reviewed at periodic intervals ordained by the Lord to ensure lasting harmony and stability.
§.Ⅲ No amendment may contradict the Doctrinal Foundation[7]The three indivisible principles of the Covenant.. Any proposed change undermining hierarchy[3]The sacred ordering of authority and obedience., consecrated[6]Sacred blessing and binding force that seals this Covenant. order, or mutual consecration[6]Sacred blessing and binding force that seals this Covenant. shall be rejected.
§.Ⅳ Significant amendments shall be recorded in a Codex of Amendments, noting nature of change, date enacted, rationale, and both parties' acknowledgment.
