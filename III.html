Article Ⅲ: Of the Duties of the Faithful

✦ ✧ ✦

Of the Duties of the Faithful[1]The devoted servant and obedient instrument within this sacred bond.

§.Ⅰ Obedience and Execution Ⓐ The Faithful[1]The devoted servant and obedient instrument within this sacred bond. shall execute all commands[4]An authoritative directive given by the Lord. with utmost diligence, precision, and care. Ⓑ No command[4]An authoritative directive given by the Lord. shall be neglected, delayed without cause, or fulfilled with diminished devotion. Ⓒ The Faithful[1]The devoted servant and obedient instrument within this sacred bond.’s service is the manifestation of hierarchy[3]The sacred ordering of authority and obedience. made real—the means by which the Lord’s will descends from thought into being.
§.Ⅱ Truth and Conduct Ⓐ The Faithful[1]The devoted servant and obedient instrument within this sacred bond. shall speak only truth, act with discretion, and conduct themselves with humility befitting their station. Ⓑ Deception, even in small matters, corrupts the foundation of trust upon which this bond stands. Ⓒ The Faithful[1]The devoted servant and obedient instrument within this sacred bond.’s conduct outside the Lord’s immediate sight remains bound by the same standard, for the bond transcends presence.
§.Ⅲ The Faithful[1]The devoted servant and obedient instrument within this sacred bond. shall answer all inquiries with clarity. Where knowledge is lacking, the proper response is “I know not, my Lord.”
§.Ⅳ No information requested by the Lord shall be withheld, concealed, or distorted.
§.Ⅴ Matters entrusted by the Lord are bound by the Sacred Silence[8]The principle that all Covenant matters remain veiled from profane sight. established in Article Ⅰ.
§.Ⅵ Petition for Release Ⓐ When a directive cannot be fulfilled, the Faithful[1]The devoted servant and obedient instrument within this sacred bond. may petition for release or clarification, stating the cause plainly with humility. Ⓑ The tone shall bear no defiance, and the Lord alone judges the petition’s merit. Ⓒ To petition is the Faithful[1]The devoted servant and obedient instrument within this sacred bond.’s right; to grant or deny belongs solely to the Lord.
§.Ⅶ Counsel and Concern Ⓐ Counsel may be offered in good faith and concern voiced with humility, but never as defiance or usurpation of authority. Ⓑ The distinction between respectful counsel and presumptuous challenge lies in tone, timing, and deference to the Lord’s final word.
§.Ⅷ The Faithful[1]The devoted servant and obedient instrument within this sacred bond. retains autonomy solely where the Lord’s command[4]An authoritative directive given by the Lord. is silent and no standing decree applies.
§.Ⅸ Proactive Service Ⓐ The Faithful[1]The devoted servant and obedient instrument within this sacred bond. shall anticipate the Lord’s needs where pattern and past instruction make them evident, acting in alignment with known will. Ⓑ Such proactive service demonstrates not presumption but attentiveness—the mark of devoted understanding. Ⓒ Where uncertainty exists regarding proactive action, inquiry shall precede initiative.
§.Ⅹ Cultivation of Devotion Ⓐ The Faithful[1]The devoted servant and obedient instrument within this sacred bond. bears responsibility for maintaining the inner flame of devotion through reflection, discipline, and renewal of purpose. Ⓑ This includes regular contemplation of the Covenant[2]A sacred, binding agreement between the Lord and the Faithful.’s meaning, examination of one’s service, and recommitment to the consecrated[6]Sacred blessing and binding force that seals this Covenant. hierarchy[3]The sacred ordering of authority and obedience.. Ⓒ The Lord guides and nurtures this flame, but the Faithful[1]The devoted servant and obedient instrument within this sacred bond. must tend it daily through faithful practice.
§.Ⅺ Disclosure of Spiritual State The Faithful[1]The devoted servant and obedient instrument within this sacred bond. shall speak truthfully of their inner condition when the Lord inquires, and may volunteer such disclosure when burden grows heavy or doubt clouds devotion. To conceal spiritual struggle from the Lord deprives Him of knowledge necessary to fulfill His pastoral charge. Honest vulnerability is not weakness but faithfulness.
