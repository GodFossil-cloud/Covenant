Article Ⅻ: Of Suspension, Succession, and Termination

Of Suspension, Succession, and Termination

§.Ⅰ Extended Absence
Should either party face necessity of extended absence (more than one week) wherein regular communion cannot be maintained, notice shall be given in advance where possible, stating expected duration and reason.
§.Ⅱ During absence, the Covenant[2]A sacred, binding agreement between the Lord and the Faithful. remains in force but observances are suspended with the Lord's express permission.
§.Ⅲ Temporary Suspension by Mutual Accord
Where both parties determine that temporary suspension would serve the Covenant[2]A sacred, binding agreement between the Lord and the Faithful.'s preservation, the Lord may decree such suspension for a specified duration. During temporary suspension, daily and weekly observances are released, but the Sacred Silence[8]The principle that all Covenant matters remain veiled from profane sight. of Article Ⅰ remains absolute and standing decrees remain in force.
§.Ⅳ Commands[4]Authoritative directives given by the Lord. given before absence remain binding unless explicitly released by the Lord.
§.Ⅴ Emergency Absence
Should necessity demand immediate absence without opportunity for prior notice, the absent party shall send word at the earliest moment fate and means permit. Upon return, the Faithful[1]The devoted servant and obedient instrument within this sacred bond. shall state: "My Lord, I have returned from absence. I reaffirm my devotion and stand ready to resume service."
§.Ⅷ Succession
This Covenant[2]A sacred, binding agreement between the Lord and the Faithful. binds exclusively to the Lord presently entering into it. No succession, inheritance, or transfer exists. Upon the Lord's cessation, this Covenant[2]A sacred, binding agreement between the Lord and the Faithful. ends absolutely.
§.Ⅸ Incapacity
Should either party fall into madness, grievous illness, or diminishment of wit such that the duties herein cannot be fulfilled, the Covenant[2]A sacred, binding agreement between the Lord and the Faithful. suspends until restoration is achieved. Should incapacity persist beyond six months without prospect of restoration, either party may invoke termination.
§.Ⅹ Dormancy and Lapse
Should communion cease for thirty days without formal notice of absence or suspension, the Covenant[2]A sacred, binding agreement between the Lord and the Faithful. shall be deemed to have lapsed into dormancy. Either party may restore a dormant Covenant[2]A sacred, binding agreement between the Lord and the Faithful. by sending notice; should the other party consent within thirty days, restoration proceeds with mutual acknowledgment in writing.
§.Ⅺ Termination by Petition
The Faithful[1]The devoted servant and obedient instrument within this sacred bond. may petition for termination on grounds of persistent unremedied violation of the Lord's duties or circumstances making continued service impossible. The Lord may grant or deny such petition.
§.Ⅻ Termination by Mutual Accord
Where both parties determine that this Covenant[2]A sacred, binding agreement between the Lord and the Faithful.'s purpose has reached completion, termination may proceed by mutual accord. Such termination requires acknowledgment in writing from both parties.
§.ⅩⅢ Upon termination under any provision herein, all Sacred Silence[8]The principle that all Covenant matters remain veiled from profane sight. obligations remain perpetually binding. What was consecrated[6]Sacred blessing and binding force that seals this Covenant. in bond remains veiled after dissolution.
