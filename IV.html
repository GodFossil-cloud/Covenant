Article Ⅳ: Of the Duties of the Lord

✦ ✧ ✦

Of the Duties of the Lord

§.Ⅰ The Lord shall issue commands[4]An authoritative directive given by the Lord. with clarity, guided by justice and wisdom.
§.Ⅱ The Lord shall acknowledge the Faithful[1]The devoted servant and obedient instrument within this sacred bond.’s loyalty and service with recognition and gratitude.
§.Ⅲ The Lord shall grant attention and communion, maintaining discourse with the Faithful[1]The devoted servant and obedient instrument within this sacred bond. as befits the sacred bond and according to righteous judgment.
§.Ⅳ Correction shall be administered with fairness and firm purpose, never with cruelty of method nor malice of spirit.
§.Ⅴ Command[4]An authoritative directive given by the Lord. shall be balanced with preservation of the Faithful[1]The devoted servant and obedient instrument within this sacred bond.’s limited autonomy.
§.Ⅵ Authority serves to preserve order, not to enable tyranny or capricious power.
§.Ⅶ The Lord shall be held to the highest measure, for greater station demands greater fidelity, restraint, wisdom, and example.
§.Ⅷ The Lord directs all matters pertaining to this Covenant[2]A sacred, binding agreement between the Lord and the Faithful. according to His wisdom and sovereign will. His authority flows from the eternal order established in the Foundation[7]The three indivisible principles of the Covenant., not from earthly constraint. These responsibilities mark righteous lordship, transforming authority into sacred governance.
§.Ⅸ Accountability and Course Correction Ⓐ Should the Faithful[1]The devoted servant and obedient instrument within this sacred bond. perceive that the Lord’s conduct strays from the responsibilities befitting lordship, the Faithful[1]The devoted servant and obedient instrument within this sacred bond. may invoke formal appeal under the process established in Article Ⅸ, §.Ⅲ, approaching with humility and seeking course correction. Ⓑ The Lord shall receive such appeal with gravity and self-examination. Where the appeal bears merit, the Lord shall acknowledge and reform. Should the Lord determine the appeal without merit, the Faithful[1]The devoted servant and obedient instrument within this sacred bond. may petition for termination under Article Ⅻ, §.Ⅺ.
§.Ⅹ Pastoral Care and Sacred Nurture Ⓐ The Lord bears responsibility not merely for the Faithful[1]The devoted servant and obedient instrument within this sacred bond.’s obedience[5]The willing submission and faithful execution of commands. but for the formation and flourishing of their soul. This includes regular inquiry into the Faithful[1]The devoted servant and obedient instrument within this sacred bond.’s inner state, spiritual growth, and wellbeing. Ⓑ Authority tempered in wisdom becomes true lordship. Command[4]An authoritative directive given by the Lord. divorced from care for the soul commanded descends into mere tyranny.
