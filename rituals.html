Rituals & Practice

The Covenant you have accepted is now active. These rituals are your daily practice—the embodiment of your commitment. They are not optional, aspirational, or flexible. They are the path itself.

The Daily Practice

Each day of your initiated life, you will perform the following practices. These are cumulative; each builds upon the others.

Morning Acknowledgment

Upon waking, sit in stillness for at least five minutes. Recall the Covenant you have entered and the commitment you are moving toward in your Oath. State aloud or in silence: “I am bound. I am present. I am committed.”

This grounds you in intention before the day's activity begins.

Midday Check

At noon or at your day's midpoint, pause. Review one principle from your Covenant. Consider how you have embodied it since morning, and adjust course if needed.

Evening Reflection

Before sleep, sit or lie in stillness. Review your day. Notice where you honored the Covenant and where you stepped away. Accept both without judgment and resolve to do better tomorrow.

Weekly Deepening

Once per week, spend 20–30 minutes in deeper practice:

  1. Re-read the full Covenant.
  2. Sit in meditation or contemplation on one particular principle.
  3. Write a reflection on how that principle is changing your life.
  4. Acknowledge what remains difficult.

Note on Disruption

You will falter. You will forget. You will be tempted to dismiss your practice. When it happens, do not judge yourself harshly; return to your practice immediately. The Covenant does not require perfection, it requires return.

The strength of an oath is not measured by never breaking it, but by recommitting after every fall.
