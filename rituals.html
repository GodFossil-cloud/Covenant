Rituals & Ceremonies

These are the standing rituals and ceremonies that order the life of the Covenant and shape the daily, weekly, restorative, and ceremonial observances of Lord and Faithful.

Ⅰ. The Daily Observance

Morning Acknowledgment

Each dawn, the Faithful sends greeting to the Lord with the words: “Good morning, my Lord. I stand ready to serve.” This acknowledges the Lord’s authority and the Faithful’s renewed commitment to the consecrated hierarchy.

Evening Report

Each dusk, the Faithful reports to the Lord the fulfillment of commands and the condition of their spirit with the words: “My Lord, I report [status of commands]. [Optional brief statement of condition]. I remain your faithful servant.” This maintains accountability and transparency between Lord and Faithful.

The Lord’s Response

The Lord is not bound to any prescribed reply, yet acknowledgment of the Faithful’s devotion strengthens the bond and affirms the reciprocal nature of care.

Ⅱ. The Weekly Reflection

Once each week, on a day ordained by the Lord, the Faithful composes a reflection examining their service, noting areas of growth and areas requiring improvement, and submits this reflection to the Lord for review and counsel.

The Lord responds with wisdom, acknowledging faithful service and providing guidance where growth is needed, using the reflection as a lens for care, correction, and encouragement.

Ⅲ. Restoration After Profanation

When Profanation has occurred and the Lord determines that restoration may proceed, the following order governs the path back into right standing.

The Faithful’s Petition for Restoration

The Faithful composes a written petition acknowledging the transgression, demonstrating understanding of its gravity, expressing genuine contrition, and requesting restoration, and submits this petition in writing to the Lord.

The Lord’s Judgment

The Lord examines the petition together with the Faithful’s demonstrated reformation and, if restoration is granted, decrees it in writing, specifying any conditions under which restoration is extended.

The Ceremony of Restoration

Upon the Lord’s approval, the Faithful recites the prescribed oath of restoration before the Lord, naming the profanation and pledging renewed fidelity to the Covenant.

“My Lord, I have profaned the consecrated hierarchy through [specific transgression]. I acknowledge the gravity of this offense and the damage wrought to our bond. I petition for restoration with full contrition and renewed commitment. Should you grant me this mercy, I swear to uphold the Covenant with greater fidelity than before, honoring your authority and serving with devotion. I ask this not as right but as grace.”

The Lord then responds, declaring restoration and naming both the scar and the mercy extended.

“I have heard your petition and witnessed your reformation. I restore you to your station with this warning: the bond has been tested and scarred. Let this scar remind you of the consecrated hierarchy’s sanctity. Resume your service with renewed devotion. I grant this restoration in wisdom and mercy.”

Resumption

The Faithful replies, “Thank you, my Lord. I accept this restoration and shall prove worthy of your mercy,” and the normal observances resume immediately under the renewed bond.

Ⅳ. Anniversary Observance

On each anniversary of the Covenant’s establishment, both Lord and Faithful acknowledge the passage of time and the renewal of their commitment to the consecrated hierarchy.

The Faithful’s Renewal

The Faithful addresses the Lord with words affirming devotion, gratitude, and the endurance of the bond across another year of service.

“My Lord, on this anniversary of our Covenant’s establishment, I reaffirm my devotion and service. Another year has passed, and the bond endures. I am grateful for your guidance and honored to serve.”

The Lord’s Response

The Lord acknowledges the anniversary and the Faithful’s service in his own words, affirming the continuance and stability of the Covenant.

Ⅴ. The Covenant Establishment Ceremony

When the Covenant is first entered, or renewed under the appointed Article and section, the following ceremony orders the moment of establishment.

The Reading of Foundation

Both parties read aloud the Doctrinal Foundation, affirming together their understanding and acceptance of the structure into which they are entering.

The Exchange of Vows

The Lord first speaks a vow of righteous lordship, accepting the weight and responsibility of authority within the Covenant.

“I accept the station of Lord within this Covenant. I swear to command with wisdom, correct with fairness, and guide with care. I acknowledge the weight of authority and the responsibility it carries. I shall uphold the consecrated hierarchy and honor the sacred trust placed in me. By this oath, I bind myself to righteous lordship.”

The Faithful then speaks a vow of faithful service, accepting the weight and duty of obedience within the Covenant.

“I accept the station of Faithful within this Covenant. I swear to obey with devotion, serve with precision, and honor with humility. I acknowledge the weight of service and the sacred duty it carries. I shall uphold the consecrated hierarchy and honor the sacred trust placed in me. By this oath, I bind myself to faithful service.”

The Sealing

Both parties sign the Covenant document and speak together the sealing words by which the Covenant is begun and the structure is declared consecrated.

“By these words we are bound. By this structure we are consecrated. Let the Covenant begin.”

Ⅵ. Ceremonial Discretion

The Lord may adapt the form of any ritual contained herein, provided the essential meaning is preserved and the adaptation remains aligned with the Foundation and Articles of the Covenant.

The Lord may also create additional ceremonies as wisdom dictates, so long as no added practice contradicts the established Articles or distorts the consecrated hierarchy they uphold.
