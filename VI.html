Of Ceremonial and Symbolic Boundaries

§.Ⅰ This Article establishes the distinction between ceremonial acts and enforceable duties. Ceremonial boundaries govern ritual acts and symbolic observances. They sanctify the bond and mark the consecrated[6]Sacred blessing and binding force that seals this Covenant. hierarchy[3]The sacred ordering of authority and obedience., but carry spiritual weight distinct from enforceable obligation.
§.Ⅱ Ceremonial consequences are symbolic or spiritual, not punitive.
§.Ⅲ Violations merit reminder or ceremonial correction only, and do not constitute Error, Breach[9]A violation of Covenant terms and sacred obligations., or Profanation unless they violate an ethical duty (see Article Ⅹ, §.Ⅲ for classification of transgressions[11]A violation of Covenant terms requiring correction.).
§.Ⅳ Ceremonial observance reinforces hierarchy[3]The sacred ordering of authority and obedience. but does not substitute for ethical duty.
§.Ⅴ Examples include ceremonial greetings, anniversary observances, formal address in ritual contexts, symbolic gestures, commemorative acts, and prescribed invocations.
§.Ⅵ Ethical duties include obedience[5]The willing submission and faithful execution of commands. to commands[4]An authoritative directive given by the Lord., truthfulness, confidentiality, proper conduct, and fulfillment of sworn obligations.
§.Ⅶ Where ambiguity exists, interpretation favors the less severe classification until the Lord clarifies.
