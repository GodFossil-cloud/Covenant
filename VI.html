Article ‚Ö•: Of Ceremonial and Symbolic Boundaries

Article VI establishes a critical distinction: ceremonial acts vs. enforceable duties. Ceremonial boundaries govern ritual and symbolic observances that sanctify the bond and mark hierarchy, but carry spiritual weight rather than punitive consequences.

This Article prevents ceremonial violations from being conflated with ethical breaches. Forgetting an anniversary greeting is ceremonially meaningful but not a Breach. Violating confidentiality or disobeying a command crosses into ethical territory and becomes enforceable. Where ambiguity exists, interpretation favors the less severe classification.
